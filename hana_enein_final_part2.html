What was your previous knowledge of HTML/CSS/Javascript?

I had no knowledge of HTML or CSS before taking this class.
I didn't technically know JavaScript either, but I did know how to code with java,
and the execution is pretty much the same.

Did you use a template or did you build your portfolio from scratch?

I used a template, the same one from the Module 4 assignment.

What was your most challenging part of your portfolio (ie, the template, artifacts, CSS, etc.)

The most challenging part of my portfolio was mastering my relative pathways.
It was a concept I'd been confused about to begin with but getting my navigation bar links to
work and all my images to load correctly required I get good at them.

How taking this class changed your view on Web Development?

This class helped me realize just how dynamic web development is. Nothing about
the mechanics of HTML/CSS/JS specifically surprised me, but what really stood out was just how
many possible ways there was to go about solving the same problem. The other thing I mean by the word
"dynamic" is how fast-changing the web development field is, with web browsers and HTML standards being constanly updated.
It became apparent to me throughout this course that web development wasn't a "one-and-done" type thing, and that having a professional-looking website was something that required constant updating.

What would you recommend to future students taking this class?

This may be obvious, but defintely to not leave assignments to the last minute like I often did. The first week especially,
as that is where I found I had the steepest learning curve and assignments were taking me way longer than anticipated.
My other peice of advice would be to get VScode. I found that it really streamlined the process and helped me be more efficient (but
thats just a personal preferance).
